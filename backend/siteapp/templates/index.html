Types of skin diseases

Here is a concise summary of major categories our tool highlights, along with hallmark signs.

Inflammatory

Acne, eczema, rosacea

  • Redness, pustules, or bumps that flare with triggers
  • Often linked to hormones, barrier damage, or immune response

Autoimmune & Chronic

Psoriasis, vitiligo, lupus

  • Persistent patches or plaques that spread over time
  • Needs lifestyle tracking plus medical intervention

Infectious

Fungal, bacterial, viral

  • Ring-shaped rashes, crusting sores, or sudden blisters
  • Early identification limits transmission and scarring

Pigment & Sun Damage

Melasma, actinic keratosis

  • Color changes on high-exposure zones
  • Sun protection and routine exams are critical

Pre-cancerous & Malignant

BCC, SCC, Melanoma

  • Non-healing sores or fast-growing lesions
  • Requires urgent dermatology evaluation

Allergy Driven

Contact dermatitis, urticaria

  • Pattern mirrors the surface you touched
  • Patch testing and avoidance calm flare-ups
